Sega Game Gear

Description

The Game Gear was one of Sega's biggest titans of the video game industry in the 90's.
It was their first attempt on the portable video game console that had the objective to compete with the GameBoy, Atari Lynx, and TurboExpress. The Game Gear has a simple Layout having in his right side only 3 buttons the A, B and Start button, a volume wheel on his side, on the left there is a D-Pad. Like all other handhelds that try to compete against the mighty GameBoy the Game Gear failed to surpass it.

History

The Game Gear or Project Mercury while in development was released in 4 different regions, Japan in 6 de outubro de 1990, Brazil, Europe and USA were released in 26 de abril de 1991, and the last place to receive the console was Australia in 1992. The Console, unlike most other handhelds, didn’t receive any redesign or upgrade design. To fight against the GameBoy, Sega decided to try to go the opposite route of Nintendo where the GameBoy tried to be affordable but not a strong device, Sega decided to make the GameGear more costly but with more game quality having an LCD screen capable of displaying colors. The Game Lifespan ended in 2001 when it finally was discontinued in United States of America.

Hardware

  • CPU: Zilog Z80 @ 3.5 MHz
  • Memory: 8kB RAM, 16kB VRAM
  • Display: 3.2-inch backlit screen
  • Graphics: 160 × 144 pixel resolution, 4096-color palette, 32 colors on-screen
  • Sounds:
    • Texas Instruments SN76489
    • Mono speaker
    • Headphone jack
  • Power: 6 AA batteries, 3 to 5 hours
  • Dimensions: 210 × 113 × 38 mm
