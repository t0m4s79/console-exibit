GCW Zero

Description

The GCW Zero is a Linux-based open-source handheld console. The software infrastructure can be found on Github.
It was designed to play games by homebrew/indie developers, as well as running emulators of classic gaming systems such as NES, SNES, Game Boy, Game Boy Advance, Playstation etc.
Thereâ€™s a special edition which sold a limited amount of 150 units. This version had a 32gb internal MicroSD instead of the normal 16gb.

History

The GCW Zero was started by the start up Game Consoles Worldwide. It started as a crowdfunding campaign on Kickstarter and it collected US$238,499 collected by 29 January 2013, originally aiming for $130,000. The project was created by Justin Barwick and eventually released later on 2013.
The GCW Zero was generally well received, seeing lots of positive reviews, mostly focused on the emulation capabilities of the console. Even today there is still a small community developing for this console.

Hardware

  • CPU: Ingenic JZ4770 1 GHz MIPS processor
  • GPU: Vivante GC860, capable of OpenGL ES 2.0
  • Display: 3.5 inch LCD with 320x240 pixels; 4:3 aspect ratio is ideal for retro gaming
  • Operating system: Linux 3.x (OpenDingux)
  • Memory: 512 MB DDR2
  • Internal storage: 16 GB, most of which is available for applications and data
  • External storage: micro SDHC up to 32 GB or micro SDXC of 64 GB (SDXC cards must be reformatted before use)
  • Connectivity: Mini USB 2.0 OTG
    Mini HDMI 1.3 out
    3.5 mm (mini jack) A/V port for earphone and analog TV-out
  • Audio: Speakers, microphone
  • Other: Accelerometer (g-sensor) and vibration motors
  • Wireless: Wi-Fi 802.11 b/g/n 2.4 GHz, can connect to access point or direct device-to-device
  • Dimensions: 143 * 70 * 18 mm
  • Weight: 8 oz / 225 g
  • Battery: 2200 mAh
