WonderSwan

Description

The Wonderswan consists of a Japanese-only handheld console released in the year 1999 by Bandai and developed by Koto Laboratory. One of the Gimmicks of this console was the capability of playing games in portrait mode landscape mode due to this the console has an uncommon set of buttons having in total 13 buttons, A, B on the left side, Sound, Start and Power on the middle right below the screen, and in the right side has 2 sets of 4 buttons the X sets that is used in both landscape and portrait mode and Y set that is used exclusively for the portrait buttons. Like all other handhelds of the time, the Wonderswan was driven to cancellation by the titan that is the Gameboy but during his lifespan, it had always 10% of the handheld market in Japan which is a feat that not many companies can brag about.

History

The history of the Wonderswan begins with Gunpei Yokoi, the mind behind many GameBoy and the GameWatch that left Nintendo out of shame due to the big failure that was the Virtual boy. In 1996 Gunpei created the engineering firm Koto Laboratory that was approached by Bandai to create a competitor for the Gameboy. The original Wonderswan was released on March 4 of 1999 with many cases color being pearl white, skeleton green, silver metallic, skeleton pink, blue metallic, skeleton blue, skeleton black, camouflage, and gold. One year and a half after the release of the original an upgraded form known as WonderSwan Color was released that ditched the monochromatic screen and upgraded it for the color scheme. The last redesign of the handheld was released July 12, 2002, known as SwanCrystal that improved the LCD screen but even with this redesign the console could compete with the Gameboy advance and it was discontinued in 2003 due to low demand.

Hardware

  • 512 kbit (64 KB) RAM
  • FSTN (WonderSwan, WonderSwan Color) TFT LCD (SwanCrystal) 224 × 144
  • 4 PCM channels
  • 1 × AA battery 40 hours (WonderSwan) 20 hours (WonderSwan Color) 15 hours (SwanCrystal)
