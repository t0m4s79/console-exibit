N-GAGE

Description

The N-Gage also known as the ‘Taco phone’ was the first hybrid of a smartphone and a handheld game system ever created.This device was created by Nokia to compete against Game Boy Advance, an objective too big for its own shoulder and it led to the cancellation of the product. The N-GAGE due to being hibrid it had a very unique button layout having the numbers buttons and other buttons that were in every phone in the 2000 before touchscreen became the norm in his right also had a dpad that were used in most games for the system. Even if this console failed in his pursuit of dethroning Nintendo from the king of the handhelds there is some merit to the N-GAGE due this having features that are mainstays of every console in the market nowadays like online multiplayer and leaderboards.

History

Nokia a Finnish Corporation that existed since 1865, during it lifespan the Nokia changed the market a lot of time until it got to the year 1990 where they entered the cell phone market and became one of the biggest companies in the market. Due to the increase of gaming, cell phone popularity an idea was created to combine the two and create a new console that can be used as both, the console was the N-GAGE. The N-GAGE was released on 7 of October 2003 to not great success being outsold by the GameBoy Advance by 100 to 1. To try to save the N-Gage from death the console received an redesign to correct most of the original console mistakes this redesign was known as N-Gage QD and it was released on May 26, 2004.Even with the N-Gage QD the console was discontinued in 24 of February 2006

Hardware

  • Media: MultiMediaCard
  • OS: Symbian OS 6.1 (Series 60)
  • CPU: ARM920T @ 104 MHz
  • Storage: MultiMediaCard, 3.4 MB internal memory (1st gen N-Gage)
  • Connetivity: HSCSD, GPRS, Bluetooth
